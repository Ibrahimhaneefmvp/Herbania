History and Origins

Unani medicine, or Yunani Tibb, traces its roots to ancient Greece (Unan means Greece in Arabic). Pioneered by Hippocrates (460-370 BC) and Galen (129-200 AD), it was refined by Arab scholars like Ibn Sina (Avicenna) in his Canon of Medicine. Introduced to India during the 12th century via Muslim rulers, it flourished under the Mughals, integrating with local herbal traditions.

Core Principles

Unani views health as a balance of four humors (Dam - blood, Balgham - phlegm, Safra - yellow bile, Sauda - black bile) corresponding to four elements (air, water, fire, earth). Imbalance causes illness. Temperament (Mizaj) is key, with treatments restoring equilibrium through natural means.

  • Seven natural factors (Umoor-e-Tabiya) govern body functions.
  • Emphasis on preventive care via diet, exercise, and environment.
  • Holistic approach addressing body, mind, and spirit.

Treatments and Therapies

Unani employs natural, plant-based remedies and non-invasive therapies:

  • Ilaj-bil-Dawa (Pharmacotherapy): Herbal medicines like arqs (distillates), majuns (pastes).
  • Ilaj-bil-Ghiza (Dietotherapy): Customized diets based on temperament.
  • Ilaj-bil-Tadbeer (Regimental Therapy): Cupping (Hijama), massage, exercise, bathing.
  • Ilaj-bil-Yad (Surgery): Minor procedures when needed.

Common for chronic issues like arthritis, diabetes, skin disorders, using herbs like aloe vera, turmeric, and licorice.

Modern Relevance

Recognized by WHO and integrated in India's AYUSH system, Unani has over 40,000 practitioners and 250+ hospitals. Research via CCRUM validates efficacy for conditions like psoriasis and vitiligo. It complements allopathy, focusing on immunity and prevention amid rising lifestyle diseases.

  • Evidence-based studies on anti-inflammatory herbs.
  • Digital libraries preserving 50,000+ manuscripts.
  • Growing global interest in natural, side-effect-free healing.
