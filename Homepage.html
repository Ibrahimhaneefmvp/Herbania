About Unani Medicine

The Ancient Healing System

Unani medicine, also known as Yunani medicine, is a traditional system of healing that originated in ancient Greece but was developed and refined in the Indian subcontinent. The word "Unani" means "Greek" in Arabic, reflecting its Hellenistic origins.

This holistic system is based on the teachings of Hippocrates and Galen, and was further developed by Arab and Persian physicians like Avicenna (Ibn Sina). It emphasizes the balance of four humors in the body: blood, phlegm, yellow bile, and black bile.

Unani medicine was introduced to India around the 12th century and flourished under Mughal rule. Today, India is one of the leading countries in the practice and development of this traditional medicine system.
